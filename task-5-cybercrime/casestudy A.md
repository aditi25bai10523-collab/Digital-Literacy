# Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime where attackers try to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted source.

In a typical phishing attack, a student receives an email that appears to be from their bank or a popular platform. The message usually creates urgency, such as claiming that the account will be blocked if immediate action is not taken. It includes a link that looks legitimate. When the user clicks on the link, they are redirected to a fake website that closely resembles the original one. Without realizing the fraud, the user enters their login credentials or banking details.

The attacker then uses this information to access the user’s account, which can lead to unauthorized transactions or misuse of personal data. Students and young users are often targeted because they may not be fully aware of such scams.

The consequences of phishing attacks include financial loss, identity theft, and emotional stress. Victims may also lose trust in online platforms.

This case study highlights the importance of being cautious while clicking on links and sharing personal information online.
